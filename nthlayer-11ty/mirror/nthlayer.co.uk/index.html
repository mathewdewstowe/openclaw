Problem

Investors and portfolio companies reach product inflection points where AI, market dynamics, and competitive change reshape how value is created, but product strategy and operating models remain anchored in a previous phase of growth. Business-as-usual delivery reinforces existing priorities, even as strategy loses alignment with where future value sits.

Operating models that once enabled scale no longer accelerate value creation. Decision-making slows, execution focuses on output over outcomes, and capital remains tied to legacy assumptions — precisely when strategic clarity and operating model change are needed to unlock the next phase of growth.

Solution

Independent product and AI leadership that helps investors and operators realign product strategy and evolve operating models at moments of inflection where AI and market change redefine how value is created. Working alongside leadership teams, strategic assumptions are challenged, priorities are reset, and execution is redirected toward initiatives that create measurable commercial impact rather than sustaining legacy ways of working.

Product strategy, decision-making, and operating models are reshaped to accelerate learning, focus investment, and improve organisational speed. Teams align around a small set of outcome-led priorities, enabling faster decisions, clearer accountability, and execution directly connected to growth, efficiency, and long-term value creation.

Why Fractional?

Fractional product or AI leadership complements, rather than replaces, a sitting CPO or CPTO. While permanent leaders focus on running the organisation, delivery, and team performance, fractional leadership is brought in to solve specific strategic or transformational challenges that sit outside day-to-day execution.

It provides independent perspective to CEOs and investors, accelerates discovery and experimentation, and injects specialised expertise — such as agentic AI, automation, or operating model change – without increasing permanent executive overhead. The result is faster progress on high-impact initiatives while core leadership maintains operational momentum.
