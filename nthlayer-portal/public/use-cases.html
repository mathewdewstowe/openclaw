01 Mid-market B2B SaaS · Cybersecurity · Europe

Feature Roadmap to
Board Level Strategy

Challenge
  • A 12-month roadmap existed, but it was operational and feature-heavy rather than driven by strategic outcomes.
  • Initiatives were not clearly linked to business metrics, making impact and prioritisation difficult to assess.
  • Alignment across leadership was inconsistent, creating friction between product, commercial, and technical decision-making.
  • Differentiation in a crowded market was difficult to articulate clearly and consistently.
  • Strategic risks were not formally defined, with no clear mitigation plan in place.
  • The product organisation was deeply embedded in delivery, making it difficult to step back and think strategically.
Approach
  • Ran a series of leadership workshops focused on strategic alignment, shifting thinking from operational execution to outcome-led strategy.
  • Created space for strategic thinking by pulling the team out of day-to-day delivery pressures.
  • Grounded discussions in market reality through a structured SWOT analysis — evidence-based rather than opinion-led.
  • Used the Airbnb 11-star exercise to push the team beyond incremental improvements and reimagine the highest-value customer experience.
  • Identified the company's true competitive advantage: depth and measurable outcomes where larger platforms offered only breadth.
  • Developed a board-ready narrative linking differentiation to business metrics.
Result

The board approved and backed the strategy, supported by a clear, outcome-focused narrative grounded in evidence-based decision-making. A board-ready strategy deck articulated a reimagined vision and defined four strategic bets designed to drive growth.

The roadmap evolved from a list of initiatives into a strategic plan tied to measurable business impact — giving the organisation a clear path to differentiation and stronger positioning against larger platform competitors.

02 Mid-market SME · Intranet / Employee Experience

Intranet to Employee
Experience Platform

Challenge
  • Extensive research including customer interviews and strategic work with EY had been completed, but lacked a clear strategic direction.
  • The organisation was drowning in research — low signal-to-noise ratio made prioritisation difficult.
  • Leadership lacked a structured way to turn insight into clear strategic choices and measurable outcomes.
  • No evidence-based plan for evolving the ICP from traditional intranet buyers toward employee experience and North American expansion.
  • Existing buyer personas reinforced legacy positioning while the market shifted toward daily engagement and employee value.
Approach
  • Ran workshops focused on the future of work, clarifying how shifting employee expectations would reshape the employee experience market.
  • Reframed strategy away from legacy buyer needs and toward driving day-to-day engagement and value for end users.
  • Increased signal-to-noise by distilling large volumes of research into a small set of clear strategic priorities.
  • Anchored decisions in commercial evidence through win/loss analysis across 3,000+ opportunities, including MEDDPICC data.
  • Defined four clear strategic bets to reposition from intranet provider to employee experience platform.
  • Built an evidence-based roadmap showing how the ICP could evolve, supporting both upmarket positioning and North American expansion.
Result

The board approved and backed the strategy, supported by a clear, outcome-focused narrative grounded in evidence-based decision-making. A board-ready strategy deck defined four strategic bets designed to drive growth.

The roadmap evolved from a list of initiatives into a strategic plan tied to measurable business impact — with a documented path to move beyond legacy buyer personas and focus investment on outcomes that mattered to end users.

03 Leading Web3 Recruitment Platform

Conversational AI to
Improve User Engagement

Challenge
  • Popular, well-established Web3 job board assets — but leadership needed to validate whether a conversational voice interface could increase engagement.
  • The core hypothesis: natural voice-based conversation would improve matching quality and response rates.
  • If successful, the approach could reduce reliance on traditional recruitment intermediaries by enabling more direct interaction.
  • The challenge was validating quickly without disrupting existing product momentum or committing significant upfront resources.
Approach
  • Built a value chain map to understand how the platform created value and where engagement gaps existed.
  • Used service design principles to identify where conversational AI could naturally fit within the user journey.
  • Defined and tested the core hypothesis that natural voice interaction would increase engagement and improve response rates.
  • Built a conversational voice AI MVP focused specifically on validating user interaction patterns and engagement behaviour.
  • Tested with real users, capturing feedback and iterating quickly to refine the experience.
  • Used continuous feedback loops to validate whether voice-driven engagement created measurable improvement before wider investment.
Result

The company gained clear strategic direction for its evolution, supported by an evidence-backed, prioritised roadmap grounded in existing research. Leadership had a documented path to reposition the offering and focus on delivering outcomes that mattered to end users.

This clarity enabled confident decision-making — allowing the team to make deliberate trade-offs and concentrate investment on the areas most likely to create meaningful differentiation.

04 High-growth B2B Startup

Building an Operating Model
for AI Transformation

Challenge
  • An AI strategy had been announced, but the product operating model remained built for predictable software rather than probabilistic systems.
  • AI initiatives were not tied to key business metrics — no clear way to measure ROI or prioritise investment.
  • Roadmaps remained feature-led, with success measured by delivery rather than commercial impact.
  • No structured approach to continuous evaluation, performance monitoring, or model tuning after deployment.
  • Ownership across product, engineering, and data was unclear — fragmented experimentation and duplicated effort.
  • Governance and risk processes were reactive, slowing progress as AI capabilities moved toward production.
Approach
  • Conducted a focused operating model review centred on commercial accountability for AI investment.
  • Defined clear value hypotheses for each AI initiative, explicitly linked to revenue, margin, cost efficiency, or retention metrics.
  • Reframed roadmaps from feature delivery to outcome-based bets with measurable success criteria.
  • Introduced ROI tracking frameworks connecting model performance to business impact.
  • Established continuous evaluation loops: live performance monitoring, human override tracking, and structured model tuning cycles.
  • Clarified ownership across product, engineering, and data with defined decision rights and accountability.
  • Integrated governance and risk guardrails into experimentation and deployment workflows.
Result

AI investment shifted from activity without accountability to initiatives directly tied to core business metrics — revenue growth, margin improvement, cost efficiency, and retention. Clear value hypotheses replaced vague experimentation, and each AI initiative was measured against defined commercial outcomes.

Continuous evaluation and tuning processes ensured model performance improved over time rather than degrading after launch. AI transformation became a disciplined, ROI-driven product strategy embedded into the operating model — not an isolated innovation programme disconnected from commercial impact.
