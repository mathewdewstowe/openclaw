The Problem

It’s not a technology problem.
It’s a decision problem.

01

More tools, more pilots, no decisions

The instinct is to buy more tools, run more pilots, build a bigger roadmap. But the constraint is never the technology. It’s the absence of a clear decision on what to build, what to stop, and in what order.

02

AI layered on broken processes just breaks faster

The question isn’t whether your systems can run AI — it’s whether your processes are worth automating. Most aren’t. Built on workarounds and tribal knowledge, layering AI on top doesn’t transform them. It exposes them.

Dead projects consume budget. Winners get starved.

No cost-per-outcome model. No visibility on what’s working. Dead projects consume budget while winning ones get starved. That’s a management problem, not an AI problem.

The Service

Product. Process. People.

AI transformation fails when it touches only one of these. The work spans all three — the product bets worth funding, the processes that have to change before AI can work, and the people and ownership structures that make it stick.

01

AI Operating Model Design

You leave with two or three funded bets, explicit kill criteria, and a clear owner for each. Everything else is explicitly deprioritised. That’s the value.

02

Agentic Infrastructure Audit & Build

Identifies what’s blocking your AI before you spend another pound on tooling. CRM, ERP, ATS — assessed and mapped. Buy, build, or rent specified for each component.

03

Product Operating Model Redesign

AI embedded in product isn’t a feature — it’s a different contract with your users. The operating model gets redesigned around non-deterministic outputs: new QA loops, human-in-the-loop design, confidence thresholds, and the accountability structures that make it shippable.

04

AI Governance & Risk Framework

Policies, accountability structures, and audit trails that satisfy boards, regulators, and acquirers. AI governance built for companies that will be scrutinised at exit.

05

AI Product Strategy

Where does AI create durable product advantage — and where does it commoditise your differentiation? The AI product bets worth making get defined: the build vs. buy vs. partner call for each, and the roadmap sequencing that protects margin as models get cheaper.

06

AI MVP Build

From validated bet to working product in weeks, not quarters. The minimum version gets scoped, built, and instrumented to prove the unit economics — so you fund the next stage on evidence, not assumption.
