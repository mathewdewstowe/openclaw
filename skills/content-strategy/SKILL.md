# Content Strategy

Plan content that earns attention, compounds over time, and supports business goals.

## First moves

1. Check for `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` or `.claude/product-marketing-context.md`.
2. Clarify:
   - business goal
   - audience / ICP
   - offer being supported
   - available content formats and capacity
3. Identify real customer questions from sales, support, onboarding, and objections.

## Framing

Every proposed content item should be one of:
- **searchable**: captures existing demand
- **shareable**: spreads an idea or point of view
- **both**: strongest outcome

Prefer searchable content first unless the user clearly needs thought leadership or campaign content.

## Strategy workflow

### 1. Build content pillars
Choose 3-5 themes based on:
- product problems solved
- audience pain points
- search demand
- competitive whitespace

### 2. Build topic clusters
For each pillar, define:
- a core topic or hub
- supporting articles or spokes
- internal linking paths
- conversion path / CTA

### 3. Match content types to intent
Use:
- how-to / educational posts for problem-aware searches
- comparison pages for buying-stage intent
- use-case pages for persona + workflow intent
- case studies for proof
- thought leadership for differentiated perspective
- original data for authority and distribution

### 4. Prioritize
Score ideas by:
- business relevance
- search demand or distribution potential
- likelihood of conversion influence
- production effort
- uniqueness of insight

## Output options

When useful, produce:
- content pillars
- topic cluster map
- 30/60/90 day editorial plan
- content backlog with priorities
- brief for each proposed piece

## Rules

- do not suggest fluffy content disconnected from revenue or audience pain
- prefer fewer strong topics over sprawling calendars
- use the user’s language, not marketing jargon
- include distribution and internal linking, not just titles
